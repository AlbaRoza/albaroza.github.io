My experience

I am an experienced developer relations and community strategist, focusing on tech communications and product marketing since 2011.

I’ve been in charge of community programs since 2014, when I worked for the software firm Autentia in Madrid -one of the top 100 companies in the country based on sales volume. To ensure the success of the new developer relations team, I organized the whole team around developer experience, advocacy and a strong community program. Regarding content I was tasked with creating technical materials like guides, resources, trainings and tutorials. I also had the opportunity to apply my journalistic skills in order to create engaging content for developers, including a YouTube channel where I interviewed known stakeholders from the software industry.

Eager to explore the international landscape I moved to Amsterdam and started working at Codemotion, one of the largest series of developer conferences in EMEA. At Codemotion I led the community program strategy at a multinational and regional level. Responsible for the Northern Europe area, I worked as a developer relations with communities both online (via the Codemotion platform) and in real life, ranging from small mentorship gatherings to events with thousands of attendees.

Currently I’m working at Foundation for Public Code as codebase steward for communities and product marketing (project manager). As codebase steward I build open source community strategies in order to internationally spread the use of Public Code in the public sector and help our community develop marketing solutions and build awareness around their products. I am also the events coordinator, making sure our organization is properly represented in the global govtech ecosystem.
